01 · Introduction

Introduction

What Is Digital Attention?

In the digital age, people's interest in urban places is reflected not only in actual visits, consumption, or commutes, but also in whether they actively search for, browse, and follow information related to that place online. In other words, urban locations possess not only physical accessibility and material visibility but also digital visibility, encompassing platform traffic, search behavior, and online reading behavior.

Core Concept

Digital visibility of people's interest in urban places can be further understood as digital attention. It refers to the observable level of public information-seeking and cognitive engagement directed toward a particular location through digital platforms, which can be measured through online traces such as pageviews over time.

Why Wikipedia Pageviews?
  • The previous studies suggest that Wikipedia data could be used to quantify patterns of public interest at scale (Mittermeier et al. 2021).
  • Its pageviews can serve as a novel digital metric of public interest (Guedes-Santos et al. 2021) or place-related interest (Owuor et al. 2023).
  • Visits to topically related Wikipedia articles can be interpreted as collective attention and viewed as a proxy for information demand (Ciampaglia et al. 2015). Therefore, Wikipedia pageviews are not merely abstract web traffic but a meaningful indicator of how urban places become visible, salient, and sought after in digital space.

Research Questions

  • How is digital attention to places spatially distributed across London?
  • How does digital attention vary over time?
  • How do the spatial patterns of digital attention correlate with socioeconomic characteristics?

Analysis

01
Overall Trend

London's digital attention exhibits a distinct U-shaped annual rhythm in 2025. Total pageviews declined from 1.90 million in January to a yearly low of 1.41 million in June, before a sustained recovery to a peak of 2.04 million in December.

02
Major Categories

Cultural & Heritage consistently closely mirrors the overall trend. Green & Recreation exhibits more pronounced fluctuations, characterized by periodic surges in interest, while Commercial demonstrates generally more moderate changes.

03
Minor Categories

In Cultural & Heritage, Church and Museum remain major contributors, but Theatre shows the strongest late-year growth. Within Green & Recreation, Park and Zoo show moderate seasonal variation, while Park stands out for its dramatic December surge. Shopping Center largely reproduces the broader commercial pattern, with high values at the beginning and end of the year and a trough in June.

04
Key Insight

Temporal change does not fundamentally reshape the geography of London's digital attention in 2025. Instead, it intensifies or weakens the existing attention hierarchy. The simultaneous decline across most categories mid-year indicates widespread seasonal weakening, while the overall rebound at the end of the year suggests a reactivation of digital attention towards highly recognizable urban destinations.

    Analysis

    01
    All Categories

    In the all-category view, digital attention is highly concentrated in central London, with Westminster dominating in 11 of 12 months. However, the hierarchy is occasionally disrupted. Tower Hamlets rises sharply in September and November, and Haringey records the most dramatic shift of the year by moving to first place in December.

    02
    Cultural & Heritage

    Digital attention directed toward this category exhibits a relatively stable core area. Specifically, Westminster consistently held the top spot. Hillingdon and Camden frequently ranked among the leaders, while Southwark, Lambeth, and Hounslow rotated positions within the top 5. However, there is a strong late-year concentration of cultural attention in Haringey.

    03
    Green & Recreation

    Green & Recreation shows a more decentralised borough structure, with Kingston upon Thames, Richmond upon Thames, and Lambeth repeatedly ranking highly, showing that attention in this category extends beyond central London. In addition, the ranking for this category is more susceptible to being reshaped by locations characterized by strong seasonality and a sudden, short-term surge in interest, such as Harrow, which may experience temporary spikes.

    04
    Commercial

    Commercial is the most rigid and concentrated ranking. It displays a dual-core structure, with Hillingdon consistently ranking first and Westminster remaining 2nd place throughout the year. It indicates that digital attention within this category is predominantly driven by a select few highly prominent and high-visibility destinations.

    05
    Key Insight

    Borough-level digital attention reflects functional specialisation and seasonal visibility. Central London provides the baseline of digital visibility, but outer boroughs become more prominent when attention shifts toward green space or when a specific POI produces a sharp temporary spike.

    Analysis

    01
    IMD (Deprivation)

    Across all POI categories, attention shows consistently very weak relationships with IMD. This suggests that Wikipedia attention is not strongly structured by socio-economic deprivation, and highly attended places are not limited to either more affluent or more deprived areas.

    02
    Population Density

    Population density also has a generally weak relationship with attention. While All POIs and Cultural categories show only minimal association, Commercial attention exhibits a slight negative relationship, indicating that highly attended commercial locations are not necessarily found in the most densely populated areas.

    03
    Public Transport Accessibility (PTAL)

    PTAL is the most explanatory variable among the three. Attention shows a moderate positive relationship with accessibility for All POIs and Cultural & Heritage, meaning better-connected areas tend to receive more digital attention. However, this relationship is much weaker for Green and Commercial POIs.

    04
    Category Differences

    Different POI types respond differently to urban context. Cultural & Heritage closely follows the accessibility-driven pattern, while Green & Recreation is more spatially dispersed and weakly structured. Commercial attention appears more selective, focusing on specific destination locations rather than general urban conditions.

    05
    Key Insight

    Overall, Wikipedia attention is not strongly explained by socio-economic status or population density. Instead, it is more closely associated with accessibility, especially for overall and cultural attention. This suggests that digital attention is not simply concentrated in wealthier or more crowded areas, but tends to align with places that are easier to reach within the public transport network.

    07 · Conclusion

    Key Findings

    Main takeaway
    London's digital attention is uneven across space and time, and does not align with the city's most densely populated or socio-economically intense areas, revealing a mismatch between digital visibility and physical urban intensity.

    What We Found

    Spatial Pattern

    Digital attention is highly concentrated in Central London, especially around Cultural & Heritage POIs, while other categories are more spatially dispersed but still shaped by a small number of prominent destinations.

    Temporal Pattern

    Over time, attention follows a clear U-shaped annual pattern, weakening mid-year and recovering toward the end, reinforcing rather than reshaping the existing spatial hierarchy.

    Socio-economic Context

    Socio-economic analysis shows weak relationships with deprivation and population density, but a clearer link with public transport accessibility, suggesting people tend to focus on places that are easier to reach rather than simply more crowded or affluent.

    Limitations

    The analysis is limited to selected POI categories with uneven sample sizes, and Wikipedia pageviews capture only one form of digital attention rather than actual visits or usage.
