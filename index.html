Analysis

01
All Categories

Digital Attention is highly uneven. The top 5 POIs are all Cultural & Heritage sites, with the leading entry reaching nearly 2 million annual pageviews.

02
Cultural & Heritage

Cultural & Heritage sites cluster densely in Central London, driving the city's overall attention concentration.

03
Green & Recreation

Green & Recreation POIs are more spatially dispersed, reaching outer boroughs at much lower pageview levels.

04
Commercial

Commercial POIs are the least numerous, concentrated around Knightsbridge and Canary Wharf, with pageview totals broadly comparable to leading green spaces.

Attention Heatmap

Wikipedia pageviews from 775 London POIs aggregated to MSOA level, visualised as a choropleth map to reveal the spatial distribution of digital attention across London's neighbourhoods in 2025.

Key Patterns
Digital attention concentrates strongly in Central London. Westminster and Camden record the highest total pageviews, while outer boroughs receive far less Wikipedia traffic.

Analysis

01
All Categories

Attention concentrates overwhelmingly in Central London, covering Westminster, Camden, the City and the South Bank. Westminster 018 records the highest pageviews at 2.2 million.

02
Cultural & Heritage

Drives the strongest and most centralised pattern, mirroring London's concentration of globally recognised landmarks.

03
Green & Recreation

The most geographically dispersed signal, with peaks in Kingston and Richmond upon Thames corresponding to major parks in west and south London.

04
Commercial

The sparsest pattern, dominated by a few global destinations: Westfield London, the West End, and Canary Wharf.

05
Key Insight

Roughly half of all 983 MSOAs receive zero Wikipedia traffic. The pattern suggests digital attention reflects symbolic and global recognition more than physical infrastructure distribution.
